Create stimuli for project on composing concrete and abstract concepts

2022-12-08

Generate a list of single words

We generate a list of concrete and abstract words by sampling from the two local maxima in the concrete rating distribution, ranging from 1 to 7, from the Glasgow Norms (Scott et al., 2018). Concrete words were drawn from the range of 2.5-3.5, while abstract words were drawn from the distribution between the range of 6-7.

All pairs of concrete-abstract words are matched on several psycholinguistic variables known to influence lexical and conceptual access: - single word frequency in Zipf (frequency per billion words; variance +-0.1) - word length (variance +-2 characters) - age of acquisition (variance +-0.1) - part-of-speech The list is further filtered based on the proportion of people knowing that word at 0.9-1. That is, we keep only words that 90% of raters know.

stim <- LexOPS::lexops %>%
    subset(PK.Brysbaert >= 0.9 & PK.Brysbaert <= 1) %>%
    split_by(CNC.Glasgow_Norms, 2.5:3.5 ~ 6:7) %>%
    control_for(Zipf.SUBTLEX_UK, -0.1:0.1) %>%
    control_for(Length, -2:2) %>%
    control_for(AoA.Glasgow_Norms, -0.1:0.1) %>%
    control_for(PoS.SUBTLEX_UK) %>%
    generate(200, "balanced", seed = 42)
